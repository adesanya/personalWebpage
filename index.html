About Me

I grew up in Lagos, Nigeria and moved to the United States in 2012 to study Computer Science at The University of California, Irvine. While pursuing my undergraduate degree I developed an interest in music, but found it difficult to learn. Determined, I hired a music tutor, “Fay Roberts”, to teach me how to play the saxophone. At the time, Fay taught music to the blind and I was fascinated by how they were able to learn. In my sessions with Fay, she applied the same techniques of tapping and moving my fingers and hands to teach me how to play. Through this, the idea of a haptic glove to help indicate what fingers to move in-order to play a musical note was born. With my technical background I was able to bring the haptic glove to life and became fascinated with wearable technologies. After completing my undergraduate degree, I joined Microsoft as a Software Engineer and continued to develop the wearable project independently. In 2017 I filed for an international patent which was later published in July 2019.

PROJECTS

Music

Haptic glove instructs wearer on what keys to play by vibrating the finger which corresponds to the musical note to be played. Over time user develops muscle memory and is able to play the song without help from the haptic glove.

Hand Tracking and Haptics

With gyroscopes and accelerometers coupled with haptic actuators positioned around the palm, the glove allows you to bring your hands into the digital world while inversely communicating your digital interactions back to you through vibrations.

AI Enabled Interactions

The glove uses a camera to recognize the object a user is pointing to using pre trained machine learning models and allows user to control the device using hand gestures. Examples include pointing to a printer to send a document to be printed or pointing to a fan to turn it on.

Palm Vision (Accessibility)

The glove uses a camera, and a depth sensor to gather information on objects or obstacles in front of the users path. It converts the data gathered to vibrations, alerting the user on proximity from obstacles to avoid collision and also provides audio feedback to describe objects recognized.

Patent

Wearable computing apparatus for augmented reality, virtual reality, and artificial intelligence interactions, and systems and methods relating thereto. Generally, a wearable computing apparatus is provided, wherein the apparatus can be worn on a user’s hand and comprises a first subassembly including one or more processors, non-transitory memory for storing instructions, at least one haptic motor, and a first set of sensors adapted to sense positional characteristics of the user’s hand. In some embodiments, a second subassembly configured to couple with the first subassembly is provided. The second subassembly can include a plurality of flexible leads adapted to attach to the user’s fingers, and wherein each flexible lead includes a haptic motor and a second set of sensors adapted to sense a plurality of positional characteristics associated with each of the user’s fingers.
